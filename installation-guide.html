Step 02

Physical placement

Where you place the Hawaa Edge matters as much as the filter itself. Give it room to breathe on all sides and keep it on a firm, level surface.

Keep 3.5 ft from walls

Maintain at least 3.5 feet (≈1.07 m) of clearance on all sides for unrestricted 360° airflow intake and distribution.

Place in the centre of the room

The geometric centre of your room gives the unit equal reach in every direction, maximising Clean Air Delivery Rate.

Level, stable surface

Set on a flat floor or low table. Avoid carpeted surfaces deeper than 2 cm, as they can restrict the bottom intake vents.

Avoid direct sunlight & heat

Keep the unit away from windows with direct sun, radiators, or cooking areas — heat skews the onboard air-quality sensor.

Do not block air inlets

Never place curtains, furniture, or clothing within 20 cm of any side panel. Blocked inlets reduce efficiency by up to 40%.

Step 03

Power connection

The Hawaa Edge runs on a standard 230 V Indian socket. No adaptor is required.

1

Remove the packaging film

Peel away the protective film from the power socket port located at the rear base of the unit before inserting the cable.

2

Insert cable into the device

Connect the device end of the power cable to the recessed inlet. You will feel a firm click when it is fully seated.

3

Plug into wall socket

Insert the 3-pin plug into any standard 230 V / 5 A Indian wall socket. Avoid extension leads longer than 2 m.

4

Press power to wake

The LED ring will glow blue during start-up (approx. 8 seconds), then shift to the current air quality colour.

Step 04

App pairing & smart setup

Download the Hawaa app to unlock schedules, air-quality history, and remote control — all from your phone.

01

Download the Hawaa app

Available on the App Store and Google Play. Search "Hawaa" and install the official app from Hawaa Technologies.

02

Create or sign in

Register with your mobile number. An OTP will be sent for verification — the same account links to hawaa.in for order tracking.

03

Connect to Wi-Fi

Tap "Add Device" and follow the in-app instructions to connect the Edge to your 2.4 GHz home Wi-Fi network.

04

Name your room

Assign the device to a room (e.g. Bedroom, Living Room) so the app can show per-room air-quality charts and alerts.

05

Set a schedule

Programme the purifier to run automatically at peak pollution windows — typically early morning and evening cooking hours.

06

Enable Auto mode

Turn on Auto mode so the fan speed adjusts automatically based on real-time PM 2.5 readings from the onboard sensor.

Step 07

Tips & ongoing care

Keep your Hawaa Edge performing at its best with these easy maintenance habits.

Replace the H13 HEPA filter every 6–12 months depending on use and local pollution levels. The Hawaa app will notify you when the filter life falls below 10%. Running the purifier in high-AQI cities (Delhi, Mumbai) may require more frequent replacement.

No. Washing or vacuuming a HEPA filter damages its fibre structure and permanently reduces filtration efficiency. Only replace it with a genuine Hawaa H13 cartridge. Do not attempt to clean the filter with any liquid or compressed air.

Power off and unplug the unit first. Wipe the exterior with a soft, slightly damp microfibre cloth. Do not use abrasive cleaners, alcohol, or spray liquids directly onto the unit. Clean the air inlet grilles monthly with a dry brush to prevent dust accumulation.

A flashing red LED indicates very poor air quality (AQI above 250) and the unit is running at maximum fan speed. Ensure doors and windows are closed. If the red flashing persists for over 30 minutes despite running at max, check that the filter is not overdue for replacement.

We recommend running the Edge continuously on Auto mode, especially in bedrooms. Indoor pollutants (VOCs from furnishings, cooking fumes, dust) accumulate overnight. In Auto mode the fan drops to near-silent speed (28 dB) when air is clean, using only 8 W of power — comparable to a phone charger.
